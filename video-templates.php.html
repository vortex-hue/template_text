Video resolution types

Video templates are available in different resolutions. A video's resolution is determined by the number of pixels organized by width and height. Based on your necessities, you can choose among:

HD (720)

720p is the lowest video resolution, often referred to as "HD." This is because it's the perfect solution for small web content. However, considering that most screens on computers today are HD, choosing a resolution higher than 720p for web browsing and streaming makes more sense.

HD (1080)

In the industry, 1080p resolutions have become the standard for clear HD video that doesn't eat up storage space. This is a common screen resolution for smartphones.

4K

4K resolution is 3840 x 2160 pixel size. If you want to view intense colors, graphics, or theatrical content, it is the best option.

Tips for choosing a video template

The process of choosing a video template will be crucial for the results you'll get. This is why it's important to take into consideration some important details.

Below we listed some tips for the right choice.

Think about your personal or business goal

It's not all about implementing a video. In other words, you shouldn't do it randomly. Rather, think about the goal you want to reach through it. For example, would you like to close more deals or attract more leads? Would you like to increase brand awareness?

The purposes may be infinite, and each of them requires a different type of video. This is why you should attentively think about it.

Know your target audience

Understanding your target audience is one of the key challenges. It not just about knowing their demographics. It's about knowing what's on their minds and what makes them take a strong step forward in the buying process.

Identify the sales funnel stage

Not every potential customer is looking for the same thing and going to take the same action. Some of them may want to get some inspiration or ideas and know more about a company; others are ready to buy straightforward. Videos can widely support this process, but they must match the buyer's intent.

There are three main stages in the sales funnel:

  • Awareness stage: this stage occurs when people don't know anything about your company and the products and services you offer. In such a case, a video can be crucial. It should be short and straight to the point.
  • Consideration stage: this middle stage occurs when people know who you are, but they look for further information. As a result, you should provide them with useful and relevant information that satisfies their curiosity.
  • Decision stage: this is the final stage. They know your business in-depth, and now they're thinking about whether to trust you or not. This is your most important chance where you need to make a good impression with a video. Some examples of videos you could use or send at this stage are case studies, testimonials, or personalized sales videos.

Quality before quantity

You won't get the results you want by placing many videos of poor quality and content. But, on the other hand, this means you should always present interesting content that gives valuable insights to your potential customer. Thus, when choosing video templates, check their resolution and whether or not they provide value.

Best uses for videos

Videos are versatile and multipurpose. You can use them for any type of content and aim.

In other words, whether for personal or business use, they'll help you achieve it. In addition, as videos are highly engaging and interactive, potential customers will be more willing to know more about your company or make a good impression on it.

Below we listed some of the best common video uses.

Advertising

Paying for ads and showing a worthless video will not be helpful. In such a case, the video needs to convince people to buy your product, service or take the action that represents your business goal (e.g., subscription or download). To help this process, make your video impactful and highlight all the benefits people will get by taking action.

Product videos

Creating product-centric videos that explain every characteristic and functionality of your product will be helpful to your potential customers. A video inspires people and helps them imagine themselves using the product and its benefits if they buy it.

App videos

Since users are often hesitant to download an app if they don't know what it is about and how it works, a quick explainer video may solving this problem. Ensure the video shows off the key features and USP that make your app better than the others.

How-to videos

Instructional videos can be really helpful for your audience. Explaining a process, how to use your product, or how your service works will remove any uncertainty or doubt your potential customers may have, convincing them to take a step forward in the buying process.

Testimonials

Sometimes the best way to convince people to buy your product or service is to show them the happy customers you already have. Hearing about a positive experience with your product or service can be the final touch that closes the deal. So make sure that the video can transmit and show the best features and aspects of your company or service.

Video Templates FAQ

What is a video template?

A video template is a sample video offered to users as a framework that they can customize according to their needs and preferences.

What’s the best program for creating video templates?

What is Adobe After Effects?

Adobe After Effects is a video editing program that allows you to create movie titles, intros, and transitions. You can also use it to animate logos, characters and add exciting effects.

What is the difference between Adobe After Effects and Premiere Pro?

Adobe Premiere Pro is used mostly for editing video and film, while Adobe After Effects is used for visual effects and text animations.
