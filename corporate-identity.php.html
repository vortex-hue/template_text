How to choose the right corporate identity template

When choosing a template for your company, regardless of the asset, you need to consider some important details that will determine its success.

To help you out, we listed below some of the best tips to choose the right template for your business.

Choose a design that reflects your brand identity

When choosing among different templates, you should make your choice based on their designs. To rephrase it, pay attention to the colors, shapes, and overall styles they present. The design options you choose should match your brand and your other digital assets, including your website.

Program compatibility

When you intend to edit the template and use a particular program, you must choose one compatible with that program. To make sure this happens, check the file format availability. For example, if you intend to use Adobe Illustrator for editing, you need to make sure that the template is available in the AI file format.

No drastic changes needed

Almost everyone prefers to buy templates rather than create their own, mainly because of the time factor and having a structured layout that only requires altering their personal information. When you select a template that requires drastic changes, it will take a longer time, in addition to paying more money to subscribe to software or hire a professional if you're inexperienced.

Responsive

Especially when it comes to digital products, you need them to be responsive. In other words, they should easily adapt to any type of screen format, and users should be able to view and read them clearly on any device. Thus, you must ensure that the template you choose has this feature.

Corporate identity materials

To build a strong corporate identity, you should identify which materials will assist you in meeting this aim.

We have listed some of the most relevant corporate identity materials below.

Brochures

A brochure is a promotional document that introduces a company, product, or service and describes its benefits to potential customers. The brochure is a powerful marketing tool that can boost your performance and business conversions.

This product works well both digitally and as a printed product. As a result, you have a greater chance of letting people know about your business.

Flyers

A flyer is a poster or single sheet posted to draw attention to business promotion, a product, an event, or a service.

Flyers are a great way to reach new clients since they're cheap, easy to make, and only printed on one side.

It is up to you how you would like to share them: online or in person, through email, in window displays, etc.

Business cards

Business cards are small cards that represent your company. As well as providing important contact information such as name, title, email, website, address, and phone number, they are often the first opportunity for consumers to see the company's overall image.

Furthermore, the logo, font, color, and texture are used to symbolize the type of industry represented.

Though you won't tell your company's entire story through business cards, they are still an excellent way to present a professional image.

Project proposal

A project proposal is a document used to define a project, whether it is internal or external. In the project proposal, you are primarily trying to get the client to decide to work with you. Thus, it can be used to win new clients or persuade executives to allocate resources to projects.

The document contains sections such as title, start and end dates, objectives and goals, requirements, and a description of the proposed solution.

Letterheads

A letterhead is the heading at the top of the paper or stationary. Typically, it contains the company logo, company name, address, and contact information.

Besides letters, letterhead is especially useful for invoices, meeting announcements, legal documents, and business-related information communications.

Well-designed letterheads act as company pads, so letter papers look much more formal and professional. Hence, regardless of its size, every business ought to have a letterhead for every important document.

Corporate Identity Questions and Answers

What does corporate identity mean?

A corporate identity is a manner in which a company presents itself to the public.

What are the essential elements of corporate identity?

The essential elements of corporate identity are logo, typography, color scheme, imagery style, design grids, and icons.

What is the difference between corporate identity and brand identity?

Brand identity refers to the perception of a particular product or service a company provides. Corporate identity refers to the perception of the entire company, not just the product or service.

What does corporate identity design mean?

A corporate identity is creating a company's visual identity and the design that goes into it.

What are corporate identity materials?

Corporate identity materials are all those visual graphic products that provide a uniform image of a business, such as business cards and brochures.
