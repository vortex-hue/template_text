Essential elements of a certificate

When creating a certificate, you should make sure that all the essential elements are included. To help you out, we've listed the most important sections of a certificate you shouldn't miss.

Title

The title should summarize the purpose of the certificate. Generally, the title is written in a larger font if compared to the rest of the text.

Most common certification titles include:

  • certificate of achievement;
  • certificate of appreciation;
  • certificate of completion;
  • certification of excellence;
  • certification of participation;
  • certification of recognition;
  • recognition award;
  • achievement award.

Presentation line

Following the title, you should include one of the following phrases or similar ones:

  • is awarded to;
  • is hereby awarded to;
  • is given to;
  • is presented to.

Recipient

The recipient section is where you’ll need to write the name and surname of the person that is going to get the certificate. You can emphasize the name somehow, such as with an italic, bold, or decorative font.

Recognition

After the recipient's name, it is customary to write one of these examples of a line of text:

  • In recognition of [description];
  • For [description;
  • Is hereby awarded this certificate of recognition;
  • Is recognized as [description];
  • Awarded for [description].

From

In the From section, you should include the name of the organization or company that is issuing the certificate.

Description

The description section is used to describe specific achievements and explains the reason for the award.

Date

The date indicates the day, month, and year on which the award is made.

Signature

The signature makes the certificate more legitimate. It’s a good practice to add the printed name beneath the signature line. Usually, it is aligned to the center or right side of the certificate.

Benefits of certification for individuals

Certifications became professional documents. Whether you attended an online course or webinar, you can show them to anyone who might be interested in your skills and knowledge or also as proof of your willingness to learn and keep yourself updated.

When getting a certificate, you consequentially enrich your resume or professional profile, which can please the hiring managers, especially when applying for a job.

Listed below are some of the most pertinent advantages of certifications.

Enhances professional credibility

Some companies and organizations want to engage only those with certain certifications that prove some specific abilities and skills. You can show your commitment to learning through certifications, which can enhance your professional credibility.

Serves as portable proof of ability

If you get a certification after attending an online course, a webinar, or any educational event, you can prove the ability or knowledge you’ve gained through it.

You can use certification to show your competencies also for any job application.

Realize a competitive advantage

Certifications can differentiate you from other applicants when applying for a job. It can give you an advantage, which is of extreme importance in today’s market.

Advantages of issuing certificates for companies

More and more companies and organizations are creating certification programs. At the end of these courses, you'll receive a certificate that shows what you learned throughout the course and what skills you developed.

If you own a business and you're looking to create a program, events, or you're looking to reward your community, a certificate is a must.

Below we listed some of the most relevant advantages of issuing certificates.

Build brand awareness

Whether your company is well-established or a startup, you can provide tangible value to your community. By attracting people's interest in your course or webinar and then issuing a certificate they can use on their social media channels (e.g., LinkedIn), you might reach their social circles, drastically increasing your brand awareness.

Enhance subscriptions and purchases

No matter whether you offer a paid certificate training program, you can use free courses and certifications to attract potential customers to your business. Through your course, they will learn more about your product or service, which increases their likelihood of buying a subscription or making a purchase.

People are motivated to take courses and act if they’ll get certifications

As the value of certification increases, more and more people are willing to attend a course or a webinar, sometimes just for the only purpose of receiving a certificate. Certificate template Questions and Answers

What is a certificate template?

The certificate template is a pre-designed document that you can send or give to someone as a token of appreciation and congratulations for achievements.

Does Google Docs have a certificate template?

Yes, Google Docs has certificate templates, among which you can choose. Even if you have a limited budget, you can choose one among the free options.

Can I issue a certificate?

Any company can create its certification programs and issue certificates with no need for accreditation.

What is a certificate of completion?

A certificate of completion is a document issued for meeting minimal occupational course, curriculum, or proficiency requirements.

Where are certificate templates in Word?

  1. Open Word;
  2. Click on New;
  3. In the Search text section, type certificate;
  4. Choose a template and edit it.
