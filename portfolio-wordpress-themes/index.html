Ten Tips for Creating Your E-Portfolio

  1. Be prepared. Select the best samples of your work. They should be well-photographed and/or have good quality.
  2. Know your audience. Do your research and include examples specifically connected to the end user's demands.
  3. Begin and finish strongly. Include compelling, distinct imagery. Stay upbeat at all times.
  4. Make it coherent and simple to navigate. Ensure your audience can quickly and easily find what they're looking for. In particular, use tabs with descriptive names, integrate your social channels, and include contact info.
  5. Seek feedback. Ask experts to review your photos and provide insightful feedback.
  6. Make frequent updates. Examine the effectiveness with which your e-portfolio showcases your skills.
  7. Track your progress. Remember that it's beneficial to demonstrate not just your most recent work but also how your talents have changed over time.
  8. Stay up to date. To keep your information current, keep an eye on market trends.
  9. Create two versions. Consider having a client-facing e-folio (or one that you will share with employers) and another version with all your samples.
  10. Promote your brand. Think of driving in traffic. Add the URL to as many of your online profiles as possible.
