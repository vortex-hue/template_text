How to choose the best CMS

When choosing the right Content Management System (CMS), you should keep in mind all your personal or business needs. However, sometimes, as there are many CMSs, the choice isn’t as easy as it seems to be.

This is why we listed below some of the most important things you should consider when selecting one or another CMS.

Ease of use

The most important thing to think about when choosing a CMS is its ease of use. Managing all the content of your website won’t be easy if the system isn’t simple.

You should ask yourself what basic requirements your website needs and how you’d like to organize and handle its content.

Thus, before making your choice, you should make sure that the CMS has all the functionalities and options that will allow you to handle every part of your website the best.

Pricing

Most of the content management systems are free, but only with their basic versions. In some cases, you may need to have full control over every aspect of your website. In such a case, you may need to pay.

Thus, before choosing a CMS rather than another, you need to consider the price you’ll have to pay and make sure it is aligned with your budget.

Customization

One of the most important things to consider when selecting a CMS is whether it will allow you to edit any spot of your website or not. Being unable to do it may be annoying and frustrating. Plus, you may be forced to hire and pay a developer if you’re inexperienced with code editing.

Security

You surely need a CMS that keeps your content, users, and visitors safe. Thus, you can’t do without one that ensures your website’s safety. Choose one with robust security protocols and features. Plus, opt for a system that prioritizes the automation (e.g., automatic backup, software updates, and so on). This way, you won’t do it manually, risking compromising the security of your site.

Benefits of using a CMS template

Buying a template instead of creating your website from scratch brings many advantages. This explains why more and more people prefer to do so. The benefits extend beyond the economic sphere.

Below we listed some of the most relevant ones you’ll potentially get.

Professional look

Creating a website from scratch requires expertise in design, other than knowledge in user experience and user interface. If you're a beginner or inexperienced, you may end up with poor results. In such a case, buying a template can be a better option for you to consider. It’ll provide your site with a professional layout and all the most important functionalities you need.

Design consistency

A template will have a consistent design, which is another advantage. Design consistency confers professionality and trustworthiness to your content, which will make people stay and interact longer with it. Plus, the design will also make your brand identity stronger, as people will be able to recognize it across the web and on social media platforms.

Time and money savings

Hiring a web developer to realize your website can be very expensive and can require a long time, based on the complexity of the project. If you’re in urgent need to launch your business, buying a template might be the right solution for you. You’ll be able to implement it immediately and, in most cases, you won’t spend more than $100.

Support

Most online templates come up with free support, which you can then extend by paying a little extra fee. This is particularly convenient as, during the implementation or use of the template, you may have issues or questions about its functionality. By contacting support, you’ll have immediate answers. As a result, you'll save the time and effort you'd have to expend if you were to figure the issue out yourself.

SEO-friendliness and responsiveness

Most of the recent templates are SEO-friendly and responsive. This means users will be able to access and clearly see and interact with your website regardless of the device they access it from. Plus, they’re SEO-friendly. For instance, they come up only with the necessary functionalities, excluding those that aren’t strictly indispensable. This means your website will load faster, and it’ll potentially rank higher on Google search results. As a result, it’ll get more exposure, traffic, and leads.

CMS Templates Q&A

What is a CMS template?

A CMS template is a pre-designed resource that shows the structure and design for any website’s comprehensive layout and display features. It is compatible with a content management system, a tool used to manage a website's content.

What is the best CMS?

WordPress is one of the most popular and used CMS. Offering a wide range of options and tools, WP makes managing a website easy and functional.

What is Drupal?

Drupal is content management software. It helps you create versatile and structured content that every website needs.
